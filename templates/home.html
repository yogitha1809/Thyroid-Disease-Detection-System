About Thyroid Disease

The thyroid gland is a butterfly-shaped gland located in the front of the neck. It produces hormones that regulate metabolism, body temperature, heart rate, growth, and energy production. When the thyroid gland produces too much or too little hormone, various thyroid disorders can occur.

Common Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Weight gain or weight loss
  • Hair loss
  • Dry skin
  • Mood changes
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Sensitivity to cold or heat

Types of Thyroid Disorders

Hypothyroidism

A condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones. Symptoms include fatigue, weight gain, depression, dry skin, and cold intolerance.

Hyperthyroidism

A condition where the thyroid gland produces excessive hormones. Symptoms include weight loss, anxiety, sweating, and rapid heartbeat.

Goiter

Enlargement of the thyroid gland causing swelling in the neck. It may occur due to iodine deficiency or thyroid dysfunction.

Why Early Detection Matters?

Early detection of thyroid disease helps prevent severe complications such as cardiovascular disease, infertility, osteoporosis, obesity, depression, and metabolic disorders. Timely diagnosis enables effective treatment and improved quality of life.

Tips for Maintaining Thyroid Health

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in iodine.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Maintain a healthy body weight.
  • Reduce stress through relaxation techniques.
  • Attend regular health checkups.
  • Take medications as prescribed by doctors.
  • Avoid smoking and unhealthy lifestyle habits.
